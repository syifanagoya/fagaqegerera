Early Life

Brian McKnight is the son of Elaine McKnight and Claude McKnight, Sr. He was born on 5 June 1969 in Buffalo, New York.

He was a member of a church choir during his childhood. He grew up as a Seventh-day Adventist. For his schooling, he went to Orlando’s Maynard Evans High School. He attended the Emmanuel Temple and has also been to the Oakwood University.

Personal Life

In 1990, Brian McKnight got married to Julie McKnight. The couple has two children named Brain McKnight, Jr., and Niko. The couple divorced in 2003. In 2017, he got married to Leilani Mendoza.

McKnight maintains a good friendship with Steve Harvey. He is a fifth-generation Seventh-day Adventists. His brother, Claude, is a member of the group Take 6.

Career

At the age of 19 years old, Brian McKnight got a recording with Wing Records, which is a subsidiary of Mercury Records. In 1992, he released his self-titled album, which became a commercial success.

His second album was ‘I Remember You,’ which was available from August 1995. In 1997, McKnight came with another commercially successful album called ‘Anytime.’ This was followed by ‘Back at One’ in 1999. This was a multi-platinum album.

In 2001, he came up with the album ‘Superhero.’ This album was released on the label Motown Records. ‘U-Turn’ became available from March 2003. In 2005, McKnight released ‘Gemini.’ This was followed by ‘Ten’ and ‘Evolution of a Man.’

McKnight then released the album ‘Just Me’ in 2011. Two years later, he came up with ‘More Than Words.’ In 2016, he released the albums ‘Better’ and ‘An Evening with Brian McKnight.’ The following year, he released ‘Genesis.’

He has also released the Christmas albums ‘Bethlehem’ and ‘I’ll Be Home for Christmas.’ His compilation albums include ‘Gold,’ ‘From There to Here: 1989-2002’, and ‘Ultimate Collection.’

Some of his popular singles include “The Way Love Goes,” “You Should Be Mine (Don’t Waste Your Time),” “Love Is,” “Crazy Love,” “Back at One,” “The Christmas Song,” and “4th of July”. He has performed as a featured artist for artists like Boyz II Men, Will Smith, Bebe, and Kyla.

Awards & Achievements

In 1998, Brian McKnight was nominated for the American Music Award for ‘Anytime.’ He has been nominated sixteen times for the Grammy Awards. In 2000, he won the NAACP Image Awards in the category of Outstanding Male Artist. He is also the winner of the 1999 Soul Train Music Awards for Best R&B Male Album. Other nominations received by him are MTV Video Music Award and BET Awards.
