Early Life & Biography

An engineer and Spanish Business, Florentino Perez, born in Madrid, was the general director of the Spanish Road Association, and later, he became a delegate of Sanitation and Environment Services of the City of Madrid. In 1983 he was appointed executive vice president of Construcciones Padrós SA.

He joined the board of directors of the Société Auxiliaire d’Entreprises (SAE), a French construction company owned by the construction group Obras de Construcción y Instalaciones S.A. (OCISA). In 1993 he was appointed President of OCP Construcciones SA and the Spanish Society of Industrial Mounts of Electricity Cobra S.A. In 1997 he was named Director of the Construction and Services Activities (ACS) group. He took over as the presidency of the club in July 2000.

Career

Florentino Perez, a student at the Polytechnic University of Madrid, joined Union of the Democratic Centre party in 1979. He later served as its secretary-general in the Partido Reformista Democrático (Democratic Reformist Party). After the fusion of OCP with Gines y Navarro into Actividades de Construcción y Servicios, S.A. (ACS) in 1997, he became President of the new company.

He was elected the President of the Real Madrid after defeating Lorenzo Sanz, who was head of the club at the time. His contributions enabled the team to gain victory for almost eight trophies, which also helped him get re-elected in the club.
